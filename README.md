Akasha Instability

Exploration of instability as a fundamental system property.

This repository was materialized by Akasha Discovery after identifying a structural sink in the phase system graph. Instability received incoming influence but had no defined propagation path, indicating an incomplete understanding of how systems evolve under pressure.

---

Purpose

Instability represents the condition where small changes produce disproportionately large effects.

This module exists to explore:

• how instability propagates through systems
• how instability transforms into other states
• when instability leads to collapse vs emergence
• how instability can be stabilized, redirected, or harnessed

Exploration Directions

This repository may examine instability across domains such as:

• physics (phase transitions, turbulence)
• economics (market volatility, crashes)
• biology (mutation, adaptation thresholds)
• computation (nonlinear systems, feedback loops)

The goal is not to eliminate instability, but to understand its role in transformation.

---

Philosophy

Instability is not an error.

It is the threshold between what a system is and what it is becoming.

Systems that cannot enter instability cannot evolve.
Systems that cannot exit instability cannot survive.
